"title": "Herb and Cheese Meatballs with a Smoky Tomato-Chipotle Sauce",
"ingredients": [
"10 large Roma tomatoes (2 lb/990 g), cored",
"2 to 5 canned chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, plus 2 tablespoons adobo sauce",
"Morton kosher salt",
"1 cup panko or fresh breadcrumbs",
"1/2 cup finely chopped fresh cilantro (1.7 oz/50 g), plus more for serving",
"1/2 cup crumbled queso fresco (2.15 oz/61 g), plus more for serving",
"1 large white onion (5.7 oz/162 g), grated on the large holes of a box grater, divided",
"5 garlic cloves, finely grated, divided",
"1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper",
"1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds",
"1 cup crema or sour cream",
"1 pound (453 g) ground beef (20% fat)",
"1 pound (453 g) ground pork (20% fat if possible)",
"3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided, plus more for shaping",
"1 teaspoon dried oregano, preferably Mexican",
"1 1/2 cups homemade chicken stock or store-bought low-sodium chicken broth"
],
"preparation": [
"Heat oven to 375°F (190°C).",
"In a large bowl, mix beef, pork, oregano, panko, 2 tablespoons cilantro, queso fresco, 1/4 cup onion, 2 garlic cloves, pepper, and cumin until just mixed. Gently form into 1 1/2-inch (4-cm) balls. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add half of meatballs and cook, turning, until evenly browned, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a plate. Add another tablespoon oil and repeat with remaining meatballs. Wipe out skillet and set aside.",
"Meanwhile, in a blender, purée tomatoes, chipotle peppers, adobo sauce, 1/4 cup onion, 
3 garlic cloves, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 cup water until homogenous. Strain through a medium-mesh sieve into the reserved skillet and discard solids. Bring sauce to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened, about 10 minutes. Add 2 tablespoons cilantro, 1/4 cup crema, and 1/4 cup chicken stock. Gently transfer meatballs to the skillet and turn to coat. Cook until sauce is thickened and lightly coats the meatballs, about 5 minutes. Taste and add more salt, if desired.",
"Transfer skillet to oven and bake until meatballs are cooked through, about 15 minutes. Remove from oven and garnish with remaining cilantro. Serve with tortillas, rice, and beans.",
],
"servings": "Serves 4"
